⚡ Renewable Energy Surges, But Grid Crisis Looms as Demand Grows and Policies Shift

Renewables Booming, but Grid Under Pressure: U.S. electricity demand is surging—NERC projects peak demand rising ~15% in summer and ~18% in winter over the next decade—driven by data centers, AI, electrification, and nearshoring.
Infrastructure & Reliability Threatened: Aging grid infrastructure, workforce shortages, and limited transmission capacity threaten reliability—DOE recently extended fossil plant operations to avoid shortfalls during peak demand.
Policy Push-Pull Complicates Transition: Federal policy shifts (e.g. rollbacks on renewables, tariffs, support for coal/nuclear) clash with clean-energy growth—solar and storage are projected to make up ~81% of new capacity in 2025, but need stable incentives.

🔋 New Jersey Seeks 1 GW of Transmission-Scale Storage

Major Procurement of 1 GW Transmission-Scale Storage: The NJ Board of Public Utilities (BPU) launched an RFP June 25 to procure 350-750 MW by October 2025, and the remainder in early 2026—part of its plan to procure 1 GW of large-scale storage within 12 months.
Supports Clean Energy Targets, No Ratepayer Cost: This effort aligns with Gov. Murphy's 2018 law mandating 2 GW of energy storage by 2030. Phase 1 is funded via existing clean-energy funds without increasing consumer rates.
Multi-Phase Program With Rolling Incentives: Phase 1 focuses on transmission-connected projects with pre-qualification due July 23 and bids due August 20, 2025. Phase 2 (2026) will address distributed (front- and behind-the-meter) storage; Phase 3 (TBD) may add performance-based incentives.

🔬 Google Signs for 200-MW Fusion Deal

Google's Trailblazing Fusion PPA: Google has signed a 200 MW power purchase agreement with Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS)—marking the first-ever commercial PPA for fusion energy.
ARC Plant in Virginia Set for 2030s Launch: The deal covers roughly half of CFS's planned 400 MW ARC fusion reactor in Chesterfield County, targeted to begin supplying power to the grid in the early 2030s.
Strategic Investment in Clean Energy & AI Demand: Google is also increasing its equity investment in CFS, aiming to support clean energy innovation as it scales data-center and AI infrastructure—echoing similar moves by Microsoft with Helion.

🏛️ Senate Passes Megabill That Curbs IRA Tax Credits, Drops Wind and Solar Tax

Senate Approves GOP "Megabill" Cutting IRA Clean Energy Credits: The Senate passed the amended budget megabill on July 1, significantly curtailing Inflation Reduction Act tax credits for renewables—removing excise taxes on wind/solar, but imposing tighter timelines for credit eligibility.
1-Year "Commence Construction" Carve-Out Added: A last-minute amendment exempts projects that begin construction within one year of bill enactment from the 2027 "placed-in-service" deadline, offering some relief amid the credit phase-down.
Bipartisan Backlash and Industry Warnings: Senators Tillis, Paul, and Collins opposed the bill; all Democrats voted against it. Clean energy advocates warn that the cutbacks and added uncertainty could suppress investment, raise electricity costs, and jeopardize grid reliability.

💰 SPP Secures Funding for Second Phase of Markets+ Development

$150M Secured for Markets+ Phase 2: Southwest Power Pool (SPP) has locked in $150 million in financing—via Simmons Bank under a FERC-approved framework—to fully fund Phase 2 of its western day-ahead and real-time market rollout.
Significant Regional Utility Collaboration: Nine (8-9) utilities across the Desert Southwest, Pacific Northwest, and Mountain West have signed onto the Phase 2 funding and participation agreements, indicating strong regional support.
Path to 2027 Market Launch: With FERC's Markets+ tariff approval in January 2025 and funding now in place, SPP will develop systems, processes, and conduct market trials—moving toward a projected 2027 go-live.
